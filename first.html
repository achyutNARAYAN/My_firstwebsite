Gaya

A city of history and culture

History

Gaya is one of the oldest cities in India, with a history dating back to the 6th century BC. It is mentioned in the Mahabharata and the Ramayana, and was a major pilgrimage site for Jains and Buddhists.

The city was also an important center of learning, and was home to the Nalanda University, one of the greatest universities in the world. Nalanda was destroyed in the 12th century, but its ruins can still be seen today.

Culture

Gaya is a city of rich culture, and is home to a number of temples, mosques, and other religious sites. The city is also known for its handicrafts, including pottery, metalwork, and textiles.

Gaya is also a major pilgrimage site for Hindus, and is home to the Mahabodhi Temple, the site where the Buddha attained enlightenment. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Things to do

  1. Visit the Mahabodhi Temple
  2. Explore the ruins of Nalanda University
  3. Visit the Gaya Museum
  4. Take a boat ride on the Phalgu River
  5. Go shopping for handicrafts
